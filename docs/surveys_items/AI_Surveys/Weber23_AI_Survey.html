Appendix A: Objective measurement scale (Weber et al., 2023)

Weber, P., Pinski, M., & Baum, L. (2023). Toward an Objective Measurement of AI Literacy. https://aisel.aisnet.org/pacis2023/60/

(n = 16 items; correct answer in italics – order randomized in test)

Socio User AI Literacy

  1. AI was first mentioned in…
    1. …the 1950s.
    2. …the 2000s.
    3. …the 1880s.
    4. …the 1980s.
  2. How are human and artificial intelligence related?
    1. They are different, each has its own strengths and weaknesses.
    2. Their strengths and weaknesses converge.
    3. They predict each other.
    4. They are the same, concerning strengths and weaknesses.
  3. AI research…
    1. …happens in an interdisciplinary field including multiple technologies.
    2. …is only fiction at this point in time.
    3. …revolves predominantly around optimization.
    4. …refers to one specific AI technology.
  4. What is a possible risk for humans of AI technology?
    1. Deep fakes render videos unattributable.
    2. Digital assistants take over self-driving cars.
    3. Image generators break the rules of art.
    4. Voice generators make people unlearn natural languages.

Socio Creator/Evaluator AI Literacy

  1. What is not an objective of current AI regulation?
    1. Enforcing a ‘no-bias policy’ to ban all potential biases that can arise from AI.
    2. Ensuring that AI systems placed on the market are safe and respect existing law on fundamental rights.
    3. Facilitating the development of a market for lawful, safe and trustworthy AI.
    4. Ensuring legal certainty to facilitate investment and innovation in AI.
  2. Which is a major regulation that has been passed specifically for artificial intelligence?
    1. European Commission’s Act for Artificial Intelligence
    2. European Regulation for Responsible AI
    3. United Nations’ Framework for the Ethical Use of AI
    4. American Regulation on the Usage of AI
  3. Which potential consequence can working with an AI have on humans that interact with it?
    1. Shift of tasks performed by humans
    2. Shift of evaluation periods
    3. Debiasing of human literacy
    4. Debiasing of result interpretation
  4. Key ethical issues surrounding AI include…
    1. …diversity, bias, and transparency
    2. …ANN, GA, and Simulated Annealing
    3. …future predictions and past overfitting
    4. …cold start problem, omitted variable trap, and sunk cost fallacy

Technical User AI Literacy

  1. What is the central distinction between supervised and unsupervised learning?
    1. Supervised learning uses labeled datasets.
    2. Unsupervised learning may happen anytime.
    3. Supervised learning is performed by supervised personnel.
    4. Supervised learning supersedes unsupervised learning.
  2. Which of the following statements is true?
    1. ML is a part of AI
    2. ML and AI are mutually exclusive
    3. AI is a part of ML
    4. AI and ML are the same
  3. What is a typical application of an AI at which it is usually better than non-AI?
    1. Image recognition
    2. Creating annual reports
    3. Undefined processes
    4. Hardware space analysis
  4. Running the same request with the same data on the same AI…
    1. …could give different results.
    2. …increase the computing speed.
    3. …never give different results.
    4. …double the computing time.

Technical Creator/Evaluator AI Literacy

  1. What always distinguishes decision trees from support vector machines?
    1. Decision trees are more interpretable.
    2. Decision trees are more implicit.
    3. Decision trees generate more predictions.
    4. Decision trees are trained faster.
  2. What is a typical split of testing and training data for development purposes?
    1. 80% Training and 20% Testing
    2. 40% Training, 40% Testing, 20% Test-Training together
    3. 95% Testing and 5% Training
    4. It does not matter
  3. What is not a strictly necessary part of a single AI system’s development process?
    1. Benchmarking
    2. Training/Learning
    3. Data preprocessing
    4. Model definition
  4. What is not part of an ANN?
    1. User Layer
    2. Input Layer
    3. Output Layer
    4. Hidden Layer
