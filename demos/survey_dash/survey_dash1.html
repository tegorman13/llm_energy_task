Literacy & Numeracy Measures

AI Literacy (Overview)

AI Literacy is assessed using dedicated scales found on the "AI Literacy Scales" tab. This includes the MAILS-SF (Koch, Carolus, et al., 2024) and scales from Rheu & Cho (2025).

Multidimensional Energy Literacy (Examples)

Items probe knowledge about device consumption, action savings, heuristics, and comparisons. Examples drawn conceptually from Attari et al. (2010), Kantenbacher & Attari (2021), Marghetis et al. (2019).

Device Energy Literacy (Estimation)

Instruction Example: A 100-Watt incandescent light bulb uses 100 units of energy in one hour. How many units of energy do you think each of the following devices typically uses in one hour? (Provide best estimate, whole numbers only)

Example based on Attari et al. (2010), Marghetis et al. (2019), Kantenbacher & Attari (2021)

Action Energy Literacy (Estimation)

Instruction Example: Turning off a 100-Watt incandescent light bulb for one hour SAVES 100 units of energy. How many units of energy do you think each of the following changes will save?

Example based on Attari et al. (2010)

Objective Numeracy (Berlin Numeracy Test - Traditional Format)

Instructions: Please answer all four questions **without a calculator**. Feel free to use scratch paper.

Source: Cokely, E. T., et al. (2012). Measuring Risk Literacy: The Berlin Numeracy Test. JDM, 7(1), 25–47.

1. Imagine we are throwing a five-sided die 50 times. On average, out of these 50 throws, how many times would this five-sided die show an odd number (1, 3, or 5)?

out of 50 throws

2. Out of 1,000 people in a small town 500 are members of a choir. Out of these 500 members in the choir 100 are men. Out of the 500 inhabitants that are not in the choir 300 are men. What is the probability that a randomly drawn man is a member of the choir? (please indicate the probability in percent).

%

3. Imagine we are throwing a loaded die (6 sides). The probability that the die shows a 6 is twice as high as the probability of each of the other numbers. On average, out of these 70 throws, how many times would the die show the number 6?

out of 70 throws

4. In a forest 20% of mushrooms are red, 50% brown and 30% white. A red mushroom is poisonous with a probability of 20%. A mushroom that is not red is poisonous with a probability of 5%. What is the probability that a poisonous mushroom in the forest is red?

%

Scoring Note: Count correct answers (0-4). Correct: 30; 25; 20; 50. Other formats (Multiple Choice, Single Item, Adaptive) and related items (e.g., Marghetis et al., 2019 subset) also exist.

Meta AI Literacy Scale - Short Form (MAILS-SF)

Instructions: In the following, you will read descriptions of different abilities that one can have when dealing with artificial intelligence. These abilities can be more or less pronounced. Please rate yourself: How pronounced are your abilities? (Scale: 0 = Not at all / hardly pronounced to 10 = Very well / (almost) perfectly pronounced)

Source: Koch, M. J., et al. (2024). Meta AI literacy scale: Further validation and development of a short version. Heliyon, 10(21), e39686.

1. I can tell if I am dealing with an application based on artificial intelligence.

Not at all / Hardly Very well / Perfectly

2. I can program new applications in the field of "artificial intelligence”.

Not at all / Hardly Very well / Perfectly

3. Although there are often new AI applications, I manage to always be "up-to-date”.

Not at all / Hardly Very well / Perfectly

4. I can handle it when interactions with AI frustrate or frighten me.

Not at all / Hardly Very well / Perfectly

5. I can weigh the consequences of using AI for society.

Not at all / Hardly Very well / Perfectly

6. I can design new AI applications.

Not at all / Hardly Very well / Perfectly

7. I can use artificial intelligence meaningfully to achieve my goals.

Not at all / Hardly Very well / Perfectly

8. I can also usually solve strenuous and complicated tasks when working with artificial intelligence well.

Not at all / Hardly Very well / Perfectly

9. I can prevent an AI from influencing me in my decisions.

Not at all / Hardly Very well / Perfectly

10. I can assess what advantages and disadvantages the use of an artificial intelligence entails.

Not at all / Hardly Very well / Perfectly

Note: Subscales are calculated as follows: AI Literacy (Detect AI: 1, Apply AI: 7, Understand AI: 10, AI Ethics: 5); Create AI (Items 2, 6); AI Self-Efficacy (Learning: 3, AI Problem-Solving: 8); AI Self-Competency (Persuasion Literacy: 9, Emotion Regulation: 4).
