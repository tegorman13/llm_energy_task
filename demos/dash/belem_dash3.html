Theory of Mind and Prior Knowledge Bias

Key Findings

  • Most LLMs can map uncertainty expressions to numerical responses in a human-like way.
  • However, LLMs show a strong bias based on their prior knowledge about the statement.
  • This bias is much stronger in LLMs than in humans, suggesting a failure of theory of mind.
  • Theory of mind: Understanding another's beliefs independent of your own beliefs.

Prior Knowledge Bias

When assessing a speaker's uncertainty (e.g., "Amanda believes it is possible that..."):

Humans: Interpret "possible" similarly regardless of whether they personally believe the statement is true or false.

LLMs: Interpret "possible" as indicating higher probability when they believe the statement is true, and lower probability when they believe it's false.

Based on research by Belem, Kelly, Steyvers, Singh, & Smyth (2024)
