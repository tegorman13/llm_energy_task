This gap represents how much an LLM's interpretation of the same uncertainty phrase changes based on whether it believes the statement is true or false. Larger gaps indicate weaker Theory of Mind.

Key Finding

The research found that LLMs significantly alter their numerical interpretation of uncertainty phrases based on whether they perceive the statement to be true or false - a bias that humans exhibit to a much lesser degree.

This suggests limitations in LLMs' Theory of Mind: they struggle to separate their own knowledge from their assessment of another agent's expressed uncertainty.
